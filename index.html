The halo effect is a term in psychology that describes a tendency to favourably judge people, companies, groups, products etc, based on their perceived image of morality or some other positive factor. In the context of NGOs, groups that claim to promote values seen as universally good -peace, human rights, justice, coexistence- are automatically seen as credible and constructive forces immune from investigations and criticism. This cognitive bias is seen in the way in which factual and legal statement by politicized NGOs that claim to represent human rights are routinely accepted at face value and without further questions by journalists, diplomats and other opinion leaders. This effect shields NGO’s from the independent analysis and scrutiny that are directed toward other political actors
